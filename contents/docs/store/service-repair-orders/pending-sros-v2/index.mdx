Pending SROs V2 replaces the original pending SRO workflow with a faster, scan-driven device intake process. Staff can scan device serial numbers to instantly create SROs in PIMS, verify customers, and print equipment tags — all from a single screen.

<Note type="warning">
This feature is in **Early Access**. You must enable it in Settings before it becomes available.
</Note>

## Enabling the Feature

<Step>
  <StepItem title="Open General Settings">
    Navigate to **Settings** → **General Settings** in Weevio Cloud.
  </StepItem>
  <StepItem title="Find Early Access">
    Scroll to the **Pending SROs** section.
  </StepItem>
  <StepItem title="Enable Pending SROs V2">
    Toggle on **"Pending SROs V2 (Scan-to-Receive Workflow)"**.
  </StepItem>
  <StepItem title="Refresh the Page">
    A notification will prompt you to refresh. Refresh your browser for the changes to take effect.
  </StepItem>
</Step>

## Batch List View

When you open Pending SROs, you see a list of all pending repair batches. Each batch represents a group of devices submitted by a customer (typically through the Customer Portal bulk repair form).

### What You See

Each batch row displays:

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Confirmation** | The repair confirmation number |
| **Customer** | Contact name (with a checkmark if verified in PIMS) |
| **Company** | Company name |
| **Email** | Customer email |
| **Phone** | Customer phone number |
| **Date** | When the batch was created |
| **Devices** | Number of devices in the batch |
| **Progress** | Visual chip showing batch status |

{/* TODO: Screenshot - Batch list view showing several batches with progress chips */}

### Batch Progress States

- **Not Started** — No devices have been received yet
- **Partially Received** — Some devices received, others still pending
- **Fully Received** — All devices in the batch have been received

### Searching and Filtering

Use the search toolbar to find batches by serial number, customer name, or confirmation number. You can also filter by batch status using the **Active** and **Canceled** tabs.

### Canceling and Restoring Batches

- Click the cancel icon on a batch row to move it to the **Canceled** tab
- Canceled batches can be restored using the restore icon in the Canceled tab

## Batch Check-In Workflow

Click any batch to open the check-in view, where you receive devices one at a time.

### Customer Verification

Before receiving devices, verify the customer in PIMS. The system automatically searches for matching PIMS records based on the customer's name, email, or customer code from the repair form.

<Step>
  <StepItem title="Review Suggested Matches">
    The verification modal shows PIMS customers that match the batch's contact information. Customers who submitted the form while signed in are labeled **"Signed-In"**.
  </StepItem>
  <StepItem title="Select the Customer">
    Click the correct customer record and then click **Confirm** to link them to the batch.
  </StepItem>
  <StepItem title="Or Search Manually">
    If no match appears, switch to the **Search Customers** tab to find the customer by name or code.
  </StepItem>
</Step>

<Note type="info">
You can change the verified customer at any time before receiving the first device. After a device is received, the customer link is locked.
</Note>

### Selecting the SRO Status

Before scanning, choose which SRO status to assign to newly created SROs using the dropdown at the top of the scan panel. Click **Save as Default** to remember your preferred status for future sessions.

### Scanning Devices

The scan input field is the core of the workflow. It accepts serial numbers from a barcode scanner or manual keyboard entry.

<Step>
  <StepItem title="Scan or Type a Serial Number">
    Place your cursor in the scan field (it auto-focuses when you open the page). Scan a barcode or type a serial number and press **Enter** or **Tab**.
  </StepItem>
  <StepItem title="SRO Created Automatically">
    The system creates an SRO in PIMS for the scanned device. A success entry appears in the activity feed showing the assigned SRO number.
  </StepItem>
  <StepItem title="Review the Activity Feed">
    The right panel shows your last 20 scan results with serial number, device type, SRO number, timestamp, and success/error status.
  </StepItem>
  <StepItem title="Repeat Until Complete">
    Continue scanning devices. The progress bar at the top updates as each device is received.
  </StepItem>
</Step>

### Handling Errors and Duplicates

- **Duplicate serial numbers** — If a serial number already exists in PIMS, you'll see a warning with the option to **force-receive** the device
- **Processing timeout** — If SRO creation takes longer than 10 seconds, a **Cancel** button appears so you can abort and retry
- **Failed scans** — Error details appear in the activity feed with a red indicator

### Equipment Tag Printing

Print equipment tags for each received device to attach to the physical unit.

- **On-demand printing** — Click the print icon next to any received device
- **Auto-print** — Toggle on **Auto-Print** in the scan panel to automatically print a tag after each successful scan

Each tag includes: SRO number, serial number, customer name, device type, and date.

## Device Table

Below the scan controls, the full device table shows all devices in the batch with their current status.

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Serial Number** | The device serial number |
| **Device Type** | Type of device |
| **SRO Number** | Assigned SRO number (after receiving) |
| **Status** | Pending or Received |

<Note type="info">
Serial numbers for pending devices can be edited before the device is received, in case the original submission contained a typo.
</Note>

## Best Practices

### For Efficient Device Intake

- **Use a barcode scanner** — Much faster than manual serial entry for high-volume intake
- **Enable auto-print** — Eliminates the extra step of printing tags after each scan
- **Set a default SRO status** — Save your most common status to avoid re-selecting it each session
- **Verify the customer first** — Always verify before scanning to ensure SROs are linked to the correct PIMS customer

### For Batch Management

- **Process batches promptly** — Receive devices soon after the customer submits them
- **Use search** — Quickly locate specific batches by serial number or customer name
- **Check progress chips** — At a glance, see which batches need attention

## Troubleshooting

### Feature Not Appearing

- Confirm **Pending SROs V2** is enabled in Settings → General Settings → Early Access
- Refresh your browser after enabling the feature
- Check that you have the correct permissions in Weevio Cloud

### Scan Not Creating SRO

- Verify the customer is linked to the batch before scanning
- Check that the serial number matches a pending device in the batch
- Ensure your PIMS system is online and accessible
- Look at the activity feed for specific error messages

### Equipment Tags Not Printing

- Check that your browser allows pop-ups for the Weevio Cloud domain
- Verify your printer is connected and online
- Try printing a single tag manually before enabling auto-print

### Customer Not Found in Verification

- Use the **Search Customers** tab to search by different criteria
- Verify the customer exists in your PIMS system
- Check for spelling differences between the repair form submission and PIMS records
